Spring Festival (Chinese New Year) · 春节

The night is loud with fireworks, red lanterns glow on every street, and families rush home for the biggest reunion of the year.

Timeline

Before Cleaning house to sweep away bad luck, buying new clothes, prepping lucky dishes.
During Reunion dinner, Red envelopes (hongbao), watching Galas, staying up late (Shousui).
After Temple fairs and the Lantern Festival ending the season.

Why it Matters

It represents a fresh start, good fortune, and the strengthening of family ties above all else.

Fun Fact:

The "Nian" monster is said to be afraid of loud sounds and the color red.

Reunion & Harmony

Mid-Autumn Festival · 中秋节

A bright full moon hangs in the sky while families gather outside with tea and mooncakes, sharing stories under the same moon.

The Experience

During the Night Family gatherings for dinner and "moon viewing." Sharing mooncakes cut into slices to symbolize unity.
Traditions Kids playing with lanterns, offerings to the moon, and sharing mooncake gift boxes with friends.

Myth & Meaning

Meaning: Gratitude and wishing loved ones well, even if they are far away.

Legend: The story of Chang’e flying to the moon is the most famous myth associated with this night.

Energy & Spirit

Dragon Boat Festival · 端午节

Drums thunder across the river as dragon boats race forward, while the air smells of bamboo leaves and sticky rice dumplings.

Action Plan

Before Teams practice racing; families prepare ingredients for Zongzi. Protection herbs hung at doors.
The Race Dragon boat races featuring teamwork, speed, and thunderous drums.

Memorial

Often linked to remembering Qu Yuan (a poet and minister), plus themes of loyalty, community spirit, and health protection.

DID YOU KNOW?

One of the most energetic festivals visually—perfect for photography.
